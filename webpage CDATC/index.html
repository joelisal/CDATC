About us

The Democratic Republic of Congo is a land filled with continental dimensions whose generous nature synthesizes the climates of the tropical world. It is a geological scandal, a gift, a divine providence that workers and peasants watch with circumspection without always tasting its flavor.
Thus, privileged beneficiaries of experiences accumulated since the beginnings of Congolese trade unionism, intellectual and manual workers, urban and rural, are unanimous in marking, from now on, a new stage of the movement.
Supporting the universal principle according to which work is an obligation, that is to say a sacred duty of men, they undertake to work because of their very humanity whose subsistence and development require work.
They must work out of consideration for their neighbors, especially for their families, for the society to which they belong, for the entire human family.
Freely and in solidarity, they proclaim the revaluation of the Congolese union by adapting it to national realities through the establishment of more modern methods of union action fundamentally focused on decentralization, consultation and clear responsibility at the base.
They spontaneously resemble each other within the: “Alarm Cries of Consumptive Workers(CDATC) in acronym for examining the future from the past and defining the means to achieve their objectives. This questioning constitutes a development of the working man resulting from the organizational capacity of society which, to do this, campaigned from the base to the top.
United within the CDATC, the workers firmly and consciously maintain that their organization is democratic and sustainable, based on dynamism, combativeness, discipline and integrity with a view to good social justice. They want it to be free from all kinds of discrimination (social, tribal, regional, gender and religion).
the CDATC, in the spirit of independence from the government of employers and political parties, is respectful of all opinions, both political and philosophical, is committed to working and establishing itself among men in a society where everyone's rights are respected.

the CDATC has the sacred mission of improving the living and working conditions of its members through collective negotiation by making the worker an artisan, a beneficiary of the socio-economic development of the company and the nation;It bases the roots and consolidation of its action on and through intensive permanent training and objective information for the orientation of its members; It is committed to giving its members and activists the assurance of solidarity in an essential collective power to protect them against injustices of all kinds; It gives meaning to families and social interests through better child protection, better education and schooling conditions, better social and health services, the establishment of a fairer, more peaceful and peaceful society. more progressive:
It intends to ensure the promotion of the social economy including non-trading activities for real purchasing power and to pay sustained attention to the most vulnerable categories of workers in both the structured and unstructured sectors, agricultural workers, self-employed workers, young workers, people with disabilities and workers at the end of their careers. Their dignity must be the subject of concern and special measures:
It is committed to fighting within the framework of national, African and international working class solidarity to contribute to the liberation and promotion of the working class in general; She reaches out to other progressive and democratic union organizations to exert influence on political institutions and finally to ensure better living conditions for those who work:
An essential factor in economic stability and progress, the CDATC is for social peace. It requires loyal and dignified behavior from its members and social partners, based on a perfect knowledge of duties and respect for rights so that peace, justice and work reign forever.
